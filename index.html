Who I am

I work in monitoring, evaluation, research and learning because I'm interested in one thing: understanding whether programmes are actually making a difference.

I enjoy asking questions, looking for patterns, and turning evidence into something people can use. Good evaluation isn't about proving success. It's about understanding what happened, why it happened, and what to do next.

More recently, I've been exploring how AI fits into that process. I don't think it replaces evaluators. I think it helps us spend less time cleaning data, writing the same reports, and repeating routine tasks, leaving more time to think, learn, and make better decisions.

How I Can Help

I help organisations build evidence they can trust and use. Whether you're designing a new programme, evaluating an existing one, or exploring how AI fits into your MERL practice, my focus is the same: build systems that produce better evidence and make it easier for people to act on it.

  • Build MERL Systems

    From theories of change and logframes to indicator frameworks and learning agendas, I design MERL systems that support accountability without becoming paperwork exercises.

  • Design Evaluations

    I design evaluations that answer meaningful questions. My work includes evaluation planning, outcome harvesting, mixed-methods research, evidence synthesis, and impact analysis that helps organisations understand what is changing and why.

  • Turn Data Into Decisions

    I build dashboards and reporting systems that make evidence easier to understand. Instead of static reports, teams get live insights they can use to monitor progress, identify challenges, and support better decisions.

  • Apply AI to Evaluation

    AI should reduce repetitive work, not replace professional judgement. I help organisations integrate AI into their evaluation workflows to improve evidence synthesis, automate routine tasks, strengthen reporting, and give teams more time for learning and analysis.
